\(w_j := w_j - \alpha\,\dfrac{\partial J}{\partial w_j}\)

Normal Equation (Analytical/Closed-Form Solution for Linear Regression)

Setup

Prepend a row of ones to \(X\) to absorb the bias \(b\) into the augmented weight vector \(\tilde{W}\):

\(\tilde{W} = \begin{bmatrix} b \\ w_1 \\ \vdots \\ w_n \end{bmatrix}_{(n+1)\times 1},\qquad \tilde{X} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & \cdots & 1 \\ x_1^{(1)} & x_1^{(2)} & \cdots & x_1^{(m)} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_n^{(1)} & x_n^{(2)} & \cdots & x_n^{(m)} \end{bmatrix}_{(n+1)\times m}\)
Model
\(Y = \tilde{W}^{\top}\tilde{X}\)
Cost / Error Function
\(J(\tilde{W}) = \dfrac{1}{m}\left\|\tilde{W}^{\top}\tilde{X} - Y_{\text{true}}\right\|_{\!F}^{2}\)
Minimising this cost is the OLS (Ordinary Least Squares) objective, scaled by \(\frac{1}{m}\) for consistency with the gradient descent formulation
Closed-Form Solution

On setting \(\nabla_{\tilde{W}}\,J = 0\) and solving the resulting system of equations, we get:

\(\tilde{W} = \left(\tilde{X}\tilde{X}^{\top}\right)^{-1}\tilde{X}\,Y_{\text{true}}^{\top}\)
Non-Invertible Case

When features are linearly dependent (multicollinearity) or when \(m \leq n\), \(\tilde{X}\tilde{X}^\top\) is not invertible. In such cases, there are two alternative approaches:

  1. Replace \(\left(\tilde{X}\tilde{X}^{\top}\right)^{-1}\tilde{X}\) with the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse → \(\tilde{W} = (\tilde{X}^{\top})^{+}\,Y_{\text{true}}^{\top}\)
  2. Implement regularisation (ridge regression) by adding \(\lambda I\) to make the matrix invertible → \(\tilde{W} = \left(\tilde{X}\tilde{X}^{\top} + \lambda I\right)^{-1}\tilde{X}\,Y_{\text{true}}^{\top}\)
Complexity & Trade-offs
Advantages No learning rate \(\alpha\) or iterations needed — one-shot solution. No feature scaling required.
Disadvantages Only applicable to linear regression. Time complexity \(\mathcal{O}(n^3)\) due to matrix inversion — very slow for \(n \gtrsim 10{,}000\). Gradient descent is \(\mathcal{O}(mn)\) per iteration and scales better.
